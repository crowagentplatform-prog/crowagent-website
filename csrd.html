What Is CSRD?

The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive is the EU's landmark regulation requiring companies to disclose environmental, social, and governance data using the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS).

Who does it affect?

Under the Omnibus I proposal, mandatory reporting applies to companies with 1,000+ employees and over €450M turnover. Medium-sized companies may opt in voluntarily.

When does it start?

Large public-interest entities are already reporting for FY2024. The revised Omnibus I thresholds apply from FY2025, with first reports due in 2026.

What must be reported?

Companies report across 12 ESRS standards covering climate, pollution, biodiversity, workforce, communities, and business conduct — using double materiality assessment.

How is it assured?

Reports must be audited by an independent assurance provider. Limited assurance is required initially, moving to reasonable assurance over time.

Double Materiality

CSRD requires companies to assess both financial impact and impact on people and the environment.

Digital Tagging

All sustainability reports must be digitally tagged in XBRL format for machine readability.

Value Chain Scope

Reporting extends beyond direct operations to cover the full upstream and downstream value chain.

Omnibus I Changes

The 2025 Omnibus I proposal raised thresholds significantly, exempting most SMEs from mandatory reporting.

Penalties

Non-compliance can result in fines determined by member states, plus reputational and financing risks.

Global Alignment

ESRS standards are designed for interoperability with ISSB, GRI, and other international frameworks.
