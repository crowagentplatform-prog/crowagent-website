FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is MEES and when does it apply?
The Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards (MEES) require commercial landlords to ensure their properties meet minimum EPC requirements before letting. The proposed Band C target for 2028 is not yet confirmed law — it is a government proposal. Current regulations (SI 2015/962) already require Band E as a minimum. CrowAgent Core helps you understand your position under both current rules and the proposed 2028 target.
How is the penalty calculated?
Penalties under SI 2015/962 Regulation 39 are based on the rateable value of the property, not a flat fee. CrowAgent Core shows you your exposure range based on your property's rateable value.
Where does the EPC data come from?
EPC data is fetched in real time from the MHCLG (Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government) Energy Performance of Buildings register. This is the official UK government data source.
What if my property doesn't have an EPC?
You can enter your property details manually — floor area, property type, construction date, and heating system. CrowAgent Core will estimate your likely EPC band and compliance position.
Can I use CrowAgent Core for residential properties?
CrowAgent Core is designed for commercial properties. Domestic EPC data is on a separate register and domestic MEES rules differ. Contact us if you have a mixed-use portfolio.
How long does a report take?
The compliance analysis runs quickly once you enter a postcode. PDF report generation typically takes 20-30 seconds. Most users get from postcode to downloaded report within a few minutes.
