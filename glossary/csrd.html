DEFINED TERM

CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive)

The EU directive setting mandatory sustainability reporting requirements for large companies — significantly narrowed by the Omnibus I package in force from 18 March 2026.

Definition

The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (Directive (EU) 2022/2464, as amended by Directive (EU) 2026/470 — “Omnibus I”) requires in-scope companies to publish annual sustainability information prepared under the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS), independently assured, and tagged in the European Single Electronic Format.

Post-Omnibus I applicability thresholds

Since 18 March 2026, the CSRD applies only to undertakings that meet both of the following criteria on two consecutive balance-sheet dates:

  • More than 1,000 employees (average during the financial year), AND
  • More than €450M net turnover

The previous thresholds (>250 employees or >€40M turnover) are superseded. According to European Commission estimates, this change reduces CSRD scope by approximately 80%.

Key facts

  • Entry into force of Omnibus I: 18 March 2026 (Directive (EU) 2026/470)
  • Reporting standards: 12 European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS), with revised versions expected 18 September 2026
  • Assurance: independent limited assurance, progressing to reasonable assurance
  • SME protection: voluntary VSME standard for smaller suppliers asked for data by in-scope companies (Article 19a(8))
  • Member-state transposition deadline: March 2027 — national law may impose additional local requirements
