25%
UK emissions from the built environment

The built environment accounts for approximately a quarter of UK greenhouse gas emissions. MEES, building regulations, and net zero targets all drive the same outcome: commercial landlords need to act on energy performance.

Source: UK Green Building Council, Net Zero Whole Life Carbon Roadmap
£65B+
Annual UK public sector procurement spend

PPN 002 requires a minimum 10% social value weighting on all central government contracts. Local authorities and NHS trusts increasingly apply the same standard, putting pressure on every supplier bidding for public work.

Source: Cabinet Office, Procurement Policy Note 06/20 and PPN 002

EU companies reassessing CSRD scope

Omnibus I raised the thresholds to over 1,000 employees and more than €450M in turnover. Thousands of groups that expected to report now need to confirm whether they remain in scope or fall below the new line.

Source: European Commission, COM(2025) 80 final — Omnibus simplification package

Our methodology

Every output traces back to its source

CrowAgent does not generate compliance opinions. The platform applies published rules, thresholds, and methodologies from the regulatory sources your team already works with.

SI 2015/962 reg 39
MEES penalty calculation

Penalty exposure uses the rateable value formula from the regulation, not a flat estimate. Short breach (<3 months) = 10% of RV. Long breach (≥3 months) = 20% of RV.

PPN 002 + Oxford SVB 2023-24
Social value scoring

Mission mapping follows the PPN 002 social value model. Proxy values come from the Oxford Social Value Bank 2023-24 edition, stored in our database and updated when new editions are published.

HM Treasury Green Book 2022
NPV financial modelling

All retrofit cost comparisons use a 3.5% discount rate from HM Treasury Green Book methodology, the standard for UK public sector investment appraisal, with a 2% energy escalation rate from Ofgem SME data.

Directive (EU) 2026/470
CSRD Omnibus I thresholds

The CSRD checker applies the post-Omnibus I thresholds: more than 1,000 employees AND more than €450M turnover. Both conditions must be met. Previous thresholds (250 employees / €40M) are superseded.

FAQ

Common questions

Is the Band C 2028 target confirmed law?
No. The Band C 2028 target is a proposed regulatory target, subject to legislative confirmation. CrowAgent Core helps you understand your position under both the current Band E rules (which are law) and the proposed 2028 target so you can plan ahead regardless of the final timeline.
Where does the EPC data come from?
EPC data is fetched from the MHCLG (Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government) Energy Performance of Buildings register. This is the official UK government data source for commercial and domestic energy certificates.
How is the MEES penalty calculated?
Penalties under SI 2015/962 Regulation 39 are based on the rateable value of the property. A breach of less than 3 months incurs 10% of rateable value (minimum £5,000, maximum £50,000). A breach of 3 months or more incurs 20% (minimum £10,000, maximum £150,000). CrowAgent never uses a flat penalty figure.
Can I use CrowAgent Core and CrowMark together?
Yes. They are separate products with separate subscriptions. If your organisation needs both MEES compliance for commercial property and PPN 002 social value for public sector bids, you can subscribe to both from the same account.
What happens after the 14-day trial?
At the end of the trial you can choose whether to continue on a paid plan. You are not charged during the trial itself, and no credit card is required to start. The CSRD Checker remains free and does not require an account at all.
Where is my data stored?
All data is stored in UK/EU data centres (eu-west-1) with AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS in transit. CrowAgent is ICO registered, GDPR compliant, and follows ISO 27001 aligned security practices. We do not share your data with third parties.
